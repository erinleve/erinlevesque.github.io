Mission

The mission of Black Students in Aerospace (BSA) at the University of Michigan is to provide Black students pursuing aerospace engineering with the resources they need to be successful in the field. We aim to build and maintain a strong academic, professional, and social community of all those interested in space and aviation related works at the University, regardless of declared major, age, and background. Additionally, we are committed to increasing visibility of Black figures within the industry by highlighting Black successes of those who came before us as well as providing representation to younger students in our community.

What We Do

Professional Development

Our organization holds a wide range of events that focus on building our members' networking and interview skills as well as the required skills to be sucessful once the are working in industry. These events include mock interviews, resume reviews, elevator pitch worksshops and more. We also host regular collaborative events with represenatives from top aerospace companies each semester in the form of panels, individual guest speakers, recruiting events, company tours, and more as an opportunity for them to share with us their experiences, advice, and sometimes one-on-one mentorship as our members navigate internship and full-time work searches.

In addition to these professional events, BSA annually sponsors our members to attend professional and research conferences by convering all registration, travel, lodging and food expenses. This allows the members of our student organization to have key networking and skill-building opportunities without financial burden.

Education-Focused Events

BSA also holds events and workshops that focus on building strategic study habits, the experiences of pursing an undergraduate and graduate aerospace engineering education, and how engineering curriculum and resources at the University of Michigan can be improved or restructured. Many of these events are in collaboration with other student organizations or University-affiliated institutions, such as the Science Learning Center and the College of Engineering Office of Student Affairs. We also hold occasional wellness study sessions in which we create a low stress space where students can gather, do homework, learn about managing stress levels with mental wellness techniques, and enjoy free food and tea in the presence of peers.

Outreach

A core value that BSA members share is the importance of creating an equitable community in which all have access to necessary resources. Particularly, BSA focuses on STEM education accessibility and has been developing workshops to introduce aerospace enginering to K-12 students from underrepresented backgrounds in the metro-Detroit area.
