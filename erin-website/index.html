For as long as I can remember I have been passionate about STEM, and I have had particular interest space-related subjects. As time passed these childhood curiosities turned into genuine interests that I realized I could make a life and career out of, so I decided to pursue a degree in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Astrophysics and Astronomy. Studying the universe and its composition will continue as long as mankind exists because there will always be more to uncover in its infinite vastness. I think the complexities of astrophysics are incredibly beautiful, and I would like to spend my university years working in research to gain new insights about this field which I hope to carry with me throughout my entire career. Upon graduation, I hope to have a career in the design, build, and test phases of space instrumentation for aerospace and astrophysics research and discovery. In my free time here on campus, I participate in the Michigan Aeronautical Science Association (MASA) student project team as the Aerodynamics subteam lead. MASA works to increase student interest in high power rocketry at the University of Michigan by providing hands-on exposure to the design, build, and test phases of engineering. On this team, I have been provided with hands-on experience with computational fluid dynamics, basic machining skills, and CAD software. The team has increased my knowledge of aerospace engineering and technologies I previously had no experience with, and I plan to work with this team until my graduation to continue learning and growing. I currently work part-time at Siemens Digital Industries Software, where I focus on high-motion computational fluid dynamics simulations with the use of the overset meshing tool. This work experience has provided me extensive knowledge on the different aerospace applications of CFD. In addition to my experience with MASA and Siemens, I have a solid educational foundation and a passion for aerospace related design. I have taken courses covering the principles of propulsion, aerodynamics, and statics and dynamics in solid mechanics, and computer programming. I am also passionate about working in STEM outreach during university and throughout my career. As one of two Black women aerospace engineers in my university, I see first-hand the need for diversity in science-related fields. I spent the summer after my freshman year teaching STEM technological skills to students in Peru to introduce them to engineering and increase interest in regions where it is uncommon. This school year, I am working to create a student organization for Black students within the Aerospace department. I look forward to a future where I can use my engineering skills and studies to bring innovative designs to my industry. After graduation, I plan to attend graduate school to obtain my Master's degree.
