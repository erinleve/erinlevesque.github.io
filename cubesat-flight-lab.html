Cubesat Flight Lab (CFL) Experience

Project Manager, Strucutres Co-Lead, MC-10 Co-Lead, Communications Subteam Member

Course Description

In this class, teams of students design, build, test, launch, and operate an industry standard prototype of a CubeSat. We learn to develop link budgets, power budgets, cost budgets, and operate ground communications stations, and analyze test and flight data. We also learn the basics of characterizing satellite performance and survivability in the space environment (vacuum, vibration, functional). We gain experience with CAD and rapid prototyping (3D printing), as well as non-technical skills such as program management and team work.

Winter 2022

In my first semester of the course in Winter of 2022, I worked as the Structures Team Co-Lead. In this role, I helped delegate tasks to the sub-team members and maintained regular communication to ensure the work was completed in a timely manner. Additionally, I developed testing procedures and ran a series of tests to see if the structure would stay in tact during unexpected impact, and subsequently designed, 3D-printed, and assembled spring-based shock absorbers to attach to the bottom of the foam thermal protective layer. The CAD for these components can be seen below. Additionally, I lead research on vibration and other oscillatory loads that the system might undergo during flight and what components might be most vulnerable to this. However, due to the short time period that the project was completed in, running an actual vibration test was not pursued.

Fall 2023

During this semester I served as one of the course's Project Managers. In this role, I managed timelines, regularly checked in with sub-team leads, and scheduled and help routine design updates throughout the semester (Preliminary Design Review, Critical Design Review, and Flight Readiness Review). Additionally, I compiled the course flight report by gathering images and documentation from each subteam.

In addition to my role as Project Manager, I also worked on the Communications sub-team. My tasks were to configure and test the APRS Byonics tracking devices, set up and write unit tests for the U-Blox NEO-M9N GPS module, and to assist in writing beacon definitions for transmitting data to ground stations from the vehicle. On day of flight, the GPS did not successfully obtain lock. Additionally, only one of the two Byonics devices on in the flight train successfully tracked the flight path. This was due to non-lithium batteries, which are not rated for low temperatures, being put into one of the devices. So, tracking was only successfully done with APRS data from a single Byonics. Winter 2023

This semester, I am currently working as the “MC-10” development co-lead. MC-10 refers to the next in-space mission that the MXL lab will develop. To lay the groundwork for the upcoming mission, my co-lead and have been delegating cost estimates and trade studies, researching NASA proposal writing, communicating to different on-campus laboratories to find interested researchers to develop a payload, and formalizing CFL testing and documentation procedures to develop a streamline from the current stratospheric vehicles to future in-space cubesats.
