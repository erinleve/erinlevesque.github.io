Astronomy and Astrophysics REU - University of Chicago

In the summer of 2019, I worked as an undergraduate researcher in the University of Chicago's Kavli Institute for Cosmological Physics as a part of a "Research Experiences for Undergraduates" (REU) program. Working under Dr. Paolo Privitera's instruction, I designed a mechanical support and designed and prototyped containment structures for ultra-high energy cosmic ray scintillator detection device. The goal was to develop a low-cost and easily accessible component, the scintillator container was 3D printed PLA, made using on-campus printers, and wrapped in layers of black paper to minimize light infiltration. The mechanical support of the detection device was a metal table that can be manually angled to all for different methods of data aquisition from a single test set-up.
