Michigan Aeronautical Science Association (MASA)

University of Michigan's Rocketry Project Team

The Michigan Aeronautical Science Association (MASA) is a project team committed to increasing student interest in high power rocketry at the University of Michigan. Our goal is to teach students about the basics of rocketry and to provide hands-on exposure to the design, build, and test phases of engineering. Since 2018, MASA has been working on the Tangerine Space Machine project with the goal of launching the first-ever student-built liquid-bipropellant rocket to the edge of space as we compete for a $1M prize. We pride ourselves on having rockets and ground support systems designed, built, and tested almost entirely by MASA members.

My work on the team has been primarily with the Aerodynamics & Recovery Subteam. I have been a member of this subteam since Fall 2018, and I served as the Technical Lead during my Sophomore Year. MASA Aerodynamics & Recovery is responsible for the design of rocket aerodynamic surfaces such as the fins and nosecone and for the design of the rocket recovery (parachute) system. The subteam also handles flight simulations and development of MASTRAN, MASA's in-house advanced 6-DOF flight simulation software. These tell the team how the rocket will behave in the air.

In addition to my work on Aerodynamics & Recovery, I have worked with production to manufacture various components for the team on the manual mill, and I worked with the ATLO (Assembly, Test & Launch Operations ) team on vibration testing.
